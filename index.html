Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. It is famous for its stunning landscapes, including the Himalayas and Mount Everest, the world's highest peak. The country has a rich cultural heritage, with Hinduism and Buddhism playing central roles in daily life. Nepal's economy is largely based on agriculture and tourism, with trekking and mountaineering being key attractions. Despite challenges such as political instability and natural disasters, Nepal continues to draw visitors with its natural beauty, spiritual sites, and warm, resilient people.

"Did You Know? Fun Facts About Nepal"

  • Home to the World's Highest Peak: Nepal is home to Mount Everest, the tallest mountain on Earth, standing at 8,848.86 meters
  • A Cultural Hub: Nepal is a mix of over 120 ethnic groups, speaking more than 120 languages, making it one of the most diverse countries in the world.
  • Birthplace of Buddha: Lumbini, located in Nepal, is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became Buddha.
  • Rich Wildlife: Nepal has several national parks, including Chitwan National Park, home to rare species like the Bengal tiger and one-horned rhinoceros.
